Who are we?

Funded by an NSF RCN-UBE grant (and defunded as part of the broader anti-DEI purge across federal science agencies), the Expanding Epistemic Boundaries in Biology Education Research (EEBBER) group convened a transdisciplinary community of educators, researchers, and makers to imagine what biology education research could become when justice, care, and lived experience are centered. In considering what form our scholarly contributions might take, the group turned to zine-making as one approach to disrupt conventional modes of knowledge production and embrace a more raw and vulnerable approach that resists the status quo.

We would like to invite you to our zine community. Contact us via email if you would like to join our virtual community or pitch a zine idea at lovingdisruptions[at]gmail.com

A collection of e-zines that explore sociopolitical issues in STEM.

In the months following our initial convening, we were inspired to create a zine that captured the transformative experience of being in community with a diverse group of scholars committed to a more just STEM. We met regularly over Zoom, using the online design tool, Canva, to collaboratively document what this experience meant to each of us through photos, drawings, cartoons, and written reflections. Our zine captured both process and product. The process included co-constructing group norms, developing shared definitions of important foundational concepts and theoretical framings, and navigating tensions that emerged from disciplinary differences and communication styles. The products included curated reading lists we hope to share with colleagues interested in exploring justice-oriented approaches to STEM education and the outline of a handbook on Critical Biology Education Research, including discussions of multiple epistemologies, diverse onto-epistemic positions, and frameworks and methodologies that could inform a more liberatory postsecondary biology research and education.

Since the creation of our first zine, Loving Disruption Vol. 1: Our Inaugural Convening, the group has gone on to publish a second volume, Loving Disruptions Vol. 2: Forbidden Knowledge. This issue centers on the theme of “knowledge considered too sensitive, dangerous, or taboo to produce” (Kempner, Merz, & Bosk, 2011). We reflected on the ways our work, which centers Black, Brown, Queer, and Trans experiences, has been further marginalized by Executive Orders issued by the Trump Administration. To further explore this theme, the group facilitated a zine-making workshop at the Society for the Advancement of Biology Education Research (SABER) East Regional meeting. The session invited participants to reflect on a recently circulated list of “trigger” or “banned” words flagged in National Science Foundation grant proposals, culminating in much needed community building during a challenging time and the collaborative creation of a long-form zine titled: BANNED.
